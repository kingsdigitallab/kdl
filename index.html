Research Themes

Our research themes expresses the overarching goals of King's Digital Lab, which align our academic strengths, that we will actively conduct studies into, to be used as a basis for meaningful and positive cultural and policy changes, to best meet the grand scientific, social and environmental challenges of our time.

Digital Humanities

an area of scholarly activity at the intersection of computing or digital technologies and the disciplines of the humanities. It includes the systematic use of digital resources in the humanities, as well as the analysis of their application.

Digital Creativity

using digital tools and technologies to explore creative ideas, apply different approaches, and find new ways of displaying your work and research.

Indigenous Heritage

Ideas, experiences, worldviews, objects, forms of expressions, practices, knowledge, spirituality, kinship ties and places valued by Indigenous Peoples. Indigenous heritage is intrinsic to Indigenous well-being and held for all generations.

Machine Learning

The use and development of computer systems that are able to learn and adapt without following explicit instructions, by using algorithms and statistical models to analyse and draw inferences from patterns in data.

Data visualisation

the graphical representation of information and data. By using visual elements like charts, graphs and maps, data visualisation tools provide an accessible way to see and understand trends, outliers and patterns in data.

Services

We cultivate an inclusive, collaborative, generative and supportive research environment to help nurture the next generation of researchers, while also supporting colleagues to gain new digital skills and engage with cutting edge research.
