Mary Chester-Kadwell is a research software engineer, originally from a humanities background. She has a PhD in the landscape archaeology and material culture of early-medieval England using GIS and computational methods.

She has worked on software projects for over twelve years, including at the British Museum and University of Cambridge. Mary has wide-ranging experience in working on large and small projects around library special collections, archival material, museum objects and digital humanities of many forms.

Mary is interested in teaching research software engineering practices and design thinking, and was a Trustee of the Society of Research Software Engineering 2023-25.
